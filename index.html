Rebar Cage Assembly with a Mobile Manipulator Enhanced by Imitation Learning

Paper

Abstract

Complex manipulation remains a main bottleneck in autonomous robotic rebar cage assembly. To address this challenge, this paper presents the first implementation of two challenging tasks: rebar insertion and rebar tying at complex intersections. A manipulation framework is proposed combining visual servoing and imitation learning (IL), which requires no depth cameras, prior modeling of rebars or the scene and is adaptable to various manipulation tasks. The proposed policies demonstrate over 90\% success rates in both rebar insertion and tying tasks in real-world robotic experiments. Furthermore, leveraging segmentation and state estimation modules yields a 33.4\% improvement in the success rate of loose insertions over the IL baseline, and demonstrates rapid adaptability across varying background conditions.
